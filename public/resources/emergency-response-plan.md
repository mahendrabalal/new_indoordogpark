## Injury Response Procedures

### Human Injury Protocol

#### Step 1: Assess the Situation
- **Immediate Action:** Ensure scene is safe before approaching
- **Call 911** if injury is serious (unconsciousness, severe bleeding, broken bones, head injury)
- **Do NOT move** injured person unless in immediate danger

#### Step 2: Provide First Aid (If Trained)
- **Minor Injuries:** Clean wound, apply bandage, document incident
- **Serious Injuries:** Control bleeding, maintain airway, keep person calm until EMS arrives
- **DO NOT** provide medical care beyond your training level

#### Step 3: Secure the Area
- Remove other dogs from the area immediately
- Have staff member direct other customers away from incident
- Preserve the scene for investigation (if applicable)

#### Step 4: Document Everything
- Complete incident report form (see Appendix A)
- Take photos of scene (if safe to do so)
- Collect witness contact information
- Note time, location, and circumstances

#### Step 5: Notify Management
- Contact manager on duty immediately
- Contact owner/operator within 1 hour
- Notify insurance within 24 hours (check policy requirements)

### Dog Injury Protocol

#### Step 1: Separate and Secure
- **Immediately separate** injured dog from others
- Move to quiet, secure area (isolation room if available)
- Keep dog calm and comfortable

#### Step 2: Assess Injury Severity
- **Minor:** Small cuts, scrapes, limping → First aid on-site
- **Moderate:** Bleeding, visible wounds, pain → Contact owner, may need vet
- **Severe:** Unconscious, severe bleeding, broken bones → Emergency vet immediately

#### Step 3: Contact Dog Owner
- **Immediate contact** for any injury
- Provide clear information about what happened
- Get permission for veterinary care if needed
- Document owner's response and instructions

#### Step 4: Veterinary Care
- **Emergency vet:** Transport immediately if severe
- **Regular vet:** Schedule appointment if moderate
- **Owner's vet:** Contact if owner requests
- **Document all veterinary visits and costs**

#### Step 5: Incident Documentation
- Complete dog incident report (see Appendix B)
- Take photos of injuries
- Document witness statements
- Preserve any evidence (torn equipment, etc.)

## Severe Weather Protocols

### Tornado/Windstorm Warning

#### Pre-Storm Preparation
- Monitor weather alerts via NOAA radio or weather app
- Secure outdoor equipment and furniture
- Close and lock all windows and doors
- Move dogs to interior rooms away from windows

#### During Warning
- **Immediate Action:** Evacuate to designated safe area (interior room, basement, or storm shelter)
- Bring all dogs to safe area
- Account for all staff and customers
- Stay away from windows and exterior walls
- Remain in safe area until warning expires

#### Post-Storm
- Assess facility for damage
- Check for gas leaks, downed power lines
- Document any damage with photos
- Contact insurance if damage occurred

### Flooding

#### Flood Watch/Warning
- **Monitor water levels** if near water source
- **Move equipment and supplies** to higher ground
- **Evacuate facility** if flooding is imminent
- **Contact dog owners** to pick up pets if time permits
- **Secure facility** and turn off utilities if evacuating

### Extreme Heat

#### Heat Advisory (90°F+)
- **Limit outdoor play time** to early morning/evening
- **Provide extra water stations** throughout facility
- **Monitor dogs for heat stress** (excessive panting, lethargy, drooling)
- **Reduce activity levels** and provide cooling areas
- **Close facility** if indoor temperature exceeds safe levels (85°F+)

#### Heat Stroke Response
- **Immediate cooling:** Move dog to cool area, apply cool (not cold) water
- **Contact emergency vet** immediately
- **Monitor vital signs** while transporting
- **Document incident** and notify owner

### Extreme Cold

#### Cold Weather Protocol (Below 20°F)
- **Limit outdoor exposure** to brief bathroom breaks
- **Ensure heating system** is functioning properly
- **Monitor for hypothermia** (shivering, lethargy, weakness)
- **Provide warm bedding** in all areas
- **Close facility** if heating fails

## Facility Issues & Evacuation

### Fire Emergency

#### Discovery of Fire
1. **Pull fire alarm** immediately
2. **Call 911** - Provide exact address and nature of fire
3. **Evacuate facility** - Use nearest exit, do not use elevators
4. **Account for all people** at designated meeting point
5. **DO NOT re-enter** building until fire department clears it

#### Evacuation Procedure
- **Alert everyone:** "FIRE - EVACUATE NOW"
- **Grab leashes** and secure dogs quickly
- **Use nearest exit** - Know all exit locations
- **Assemble at meeting point** (designated safe area)
- **Account for all staff and customers**
- **Do NOT attempt to save property** - Life safety first

#### Fire Prevention
- Monthly fire extinguisher inspections
- Quarterly fire drill practice
- Annual fire safety training for all staff
- Clear exit paths at all times
- No smoking policy strictly enforced

### Gas Leak

#### Detection
- **Smell of gas** (rotten egg odor)
- **Hissing sound** from gas line
- **Dead vegetation** near gas line

#### Response
1. **DO NOT** use electrical switches, phones, or lighters
2. **Evacuate immediately** - Do not turn lights on/off
3. **Call 911** from safe location
4. **Call gas company** emergency line
5. **Do NOT re-enter** until cleared by professionals

### Structural Damage

#### Signs of Concern
- Cracks in walls or foundation
- Sagging ceilings or floors
- Water damage or leaks
- Unusual sounds or vibrations

#### Response
1. **Evacuate affected area** immediately
2. **Assess safety** of remaining areas
3. **Contact structural engineer** if major concern
4. **Close facility** if safety is compromised
5. **Notify insurance** and document damage

### Power Outage

#### Immediate Actions
- **Check circuit breakers** - Reset if tripped
- **Call utility company** to report outage
- **Assess duration** - If extended, consider closure
- **Use backup lighting** (flashlights, battery lanterns)
- **Monitor temperature** - Critical for dog safety

#### Extended Outage (>2 hours)
- **Contact dog owners** to pick up pets
- **Maintain temperature** - Use generator if available
- **Provide water** - Ensure water systems work
- **Close facility** if conditions become unsafe
- **Document outage** and impact

## Dog-Related Incidents

### Dog Fight

#### Immediate Response
1. **DO NOT** use hands to separate dogs
2. **Use distraction** - Loud noise, water spray, blanket
3. **Separate dogs** using barriers or leashes
4. **Assess injuries** to both dogs
5. **Secure both dogs** in separate areas

#### Post-Incident
- **Document incident** thoroughly
- **Contact both dog owners** immediately
- **Provide veterinary care** if needed
- **Review incident** with staff
- **Update dog compatibility records**

### Aggressive Behavior

#### Warning Signs
- Growling, snarling, or baring teeth
- Stiff body posture
- Raised hackles
- Intense staring

#### Response
- **Remove dog** from situation immediately
- **Place in isolation** area
- **Contact owner** to pick up
- **Document behavior** for future reference
- **May require** behavior assessment before return

### Escape/Containment Failure

#### Immediate Actions
1. **Alert all staff** immediately
2. **Secure remaining dogs** in facility
3. **Search immediate area** (parking lot, nearby streets)
4. **Contact dog owner** immediately
5. **Contact animal control** if not found quickly
6. **Post on social media** and local lost pet groups

#### Prevention
- Regular fence/barrier inspections
- Secure entry/exit procedures
- Staff training on containment protocols
- Leash requirements strictly enforced

## Medical Emergency Procedures

### Unconscious Person

1. **Check for responsiveness** - Tap shoulder, call name
2. **Call 911 immediately**
3. **Check for breathing** - Look, listen, feel
4. **Begin CPR** if trained and not breathing
5. **Use AED** if available and trained
6. **Stay with person** until EMS arrives

### Choking

1. **Ask "Are you choking?"** - If can speak, encourage coughing
2. **Perform Heimlich maneuver** if cannot speak
3. **Call 911** if obstruction not cleared
4. **Continue attempts** until help arrives or cleared

### Severe Allergic Reaction

1. **Identify symptoms** - Difficulty breathing, swelling, hives
2. **Call 911 immediately**
3. **Check for epinephrine** auto-injector (if available)
4. **Assist with administration** if person cannot
5. **Monitor until EMS arrives**

## Communication Protocols

### During Emergency

#### Internal Communication
- **Designate spokesperson** - One person communicates with emergency services
- **Use clear, calm language** - Avoid panic
- **Follow chain of command** - Manager → Owner → Insurance

#### Customer Communication
- **Be honest and transparent** - Don't hide information
- **Provide regular updates** - Keep customers informed
- **Protect privacy** - Don't share personal information about injured parties

#### Media Communication
- **Refer all media** to owner/designated spokesperson
- **Do NOT speculate** on causes or blame
- **Stick to facts** - "We are cooperating with authorities"
- **Protect privacy** - No names or personal details

### Post-Emergency Communication

#### Within 24 Hours
- **Contact affected parties** - Customers, staff, dog owners
- **Notify insurance** - Follow policy requirements
- **File incident reports** - Complete all documentation
- **Internal review** - Staff meeting to discuss incident

#### Follow-Up
- **Check on injured parties** - Human and animal
- **Provide updates** as situation develops
- **Address concerns** from customers and staff
- **Implement improvements** based on lessons learned
