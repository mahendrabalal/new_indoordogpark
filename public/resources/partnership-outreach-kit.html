Partnership Outreach Kit

✨ How to Use This Kit

  1. Choose your template - Pick the pitch script that fits your partnership type
  2. Customize it - Fill in your park name, details, and specific benefits
  3. Reach out - Send via email, LinkedIn, or in-person meeting
  4. Follow up - Use the follow-up template if you don't hear back

📧 Email Pitch Scripts

1. Local Business Partnership (Pet Stores, Groomers, Vets)

Subject: Partnership Opportunity - [Your Park Name] & [Their Business Name]


Hi [Name],

I'm reaching out from [Your Park Name], an indoor dog park in [City]. We're looking to partner with local pet businesses to create value for both our customers and yours.

Here's what we're offering:

  • Cross-promotion to our [X] active members
  • Featured placement on our website and social media
  • Co-hosted events and workshops
  • Referral discounts for mutual customers

What we're looking for:

  • [Specific ask - e.g., "Discount for our members", "Co-marketing", "Product samples"]

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call to explore how we can work together?

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Your Park Name]
[Phone] | [Email]

Hello [Name],

Sponsorship Benefits:

  • Product sampling opportunities

Investment Levels:

  • Bronze: $[X]/month - Logo on website, social mentions
  • Silver: $[X]/month - Above + signage, product sampling
  • Gold: $[X]/month - Above + event sponsorship, featured content

I'd love to discuss how [Brand Name] can reach our engaged pet-owner community. Are you available for a call this week?

Thank you,
[Your Name]
[Your Park Name]

3. Community Organization (Rescue Groups, Training Centers)

Subject: Collaboration Opportunity - Supporting [Organization Name]


Hi [Name],

I'm [Your Name] from [Your Park Name]. We're passionate about supporting the local pet community and would love to explore ways we can help [Organization Name] achieve its mission.

Potential Collaboration Ideas:

  • Host adoption events at our facility
  • Donate space for training sessions or workshops
  • Co-host fundraising events
  • Feature adoptable dogs on our social media
  • Offer discounted or free access for your program participants

We're flexible and open to ideas that work for both organizations. Would you be interested in meeting to discuss possibilities?

Looking forward to connecting,
[Your Name]
[Your Park Name]

4. Follow-Up Email (If No Response)

Subject: Re: Partnership Opportunity - [Your Park Name]


Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on my previous email about a potential partnership between [Your Park Name] and [Their Business].

I know you're busy, so I wanted to make this easy. Here's a quick summary:

  • What we offer: [Brief benefit - e.g., "Access to 500+ active dog owners"]
  • What we're looking for: [Brief ask]
  • Time commitment: Minimal - we handle most of the work

If this isn't the right time, no problem! But if you're interested, I'd love a quick 10-minute call.

Best,
[Your Name]

💡 Partnership Ideas

Quick Win Partnerships

  • Pet Groomers: "Bring your dog to play, get 10% off grooming next door"
  • Pet Stores: "Show your park membership, get 15% off supplies"
  • Veterinarians: "Referral program - new vet clients get free park day"
  • Dog Trainers: "Co-host training workshops at the park"
  • Pet Photographers: "Photo sessions at the park, split revenue"
  • Dog Food Brands: "Product sampling + member discounts"
  • Pet Insurance: "Educational workshops + referral program"

✅ Outreach Checklist

  • ☐ Research potential partner (visit website, check social media)
  • ☐ Find the right contact person (owner, marketing manager, etc.)
  • ☐ Customize the pitch script with specific details
  • ☐ Prepare your "elevator pitch" (30-second summary)
  • ☐ Have your numbers ready (member count, monthly visitors, social followers)
  • ☐ Set a follow-up reminder (if no response in 1 week)
  • ☐ Be ready to negotiate terms
  • ☐ Have a simple agreement template ready

📊 Tracking Your Outreach

Keep a simple spreadsheet to track:

  • Company Name
  • Contact Person
  • Date Contacted
  • Response Status (No response / Interested / Not interested / In discussion)
  • Follow-Up Date
  • Notes

This helps you stay organized and follow up at the right time.

Customize these templates to match your park's unique value proposition and partnership goals.

Remember: The best partnerships are win-win. Focus on what value you can provide, not just what you want to receive.
