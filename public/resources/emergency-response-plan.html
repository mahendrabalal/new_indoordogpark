2. Injury Response Procedures

Human Injury Protocol

Step 1: Assess the Situation
  • Immediate Action: Ensure scene is safe before approaching
  • Call 911 if injury is serious (unconsciousness, severe bleeding, broken bones, head injury)
  • Do NOT move injured person unless in immediate danger

Step 2: Provide First Aid (If Trained)

  • Minor Injuries: Clean wound, apply bandage, document incident
  • Serious Injuries: Control bleeding, maintain airway, keep person calm until EMS arrives
  • DO NOT provide medical care beyond your training level

Step 3: Secure the Area

  • Remove other dogs from the area immediately
  • Have staff member direct other customers away from incident
  • Preserve the scene for investigation (if applicable)

Step 4: Document Everything

  • Complete incident report form
  • Take photos of scene (if safe to do so)
  • Collect witness contact information
  • Note time, location, and circumstances

Step 5: Notify Management

  • Contact manager on duty immediately
  • Contact owner/operator within 1 hour
  • Notify insurance within 24 hours (check policy requirements)

Dog Injury Protocol

Step 1: Separate and Secure

  • Immediately separate injured dog from others
  • Move to quiet, secure area (isolation room if available)
  • Keep dog calm and comfortable

Step 2: Assess Injury Severity

  • Minor: Small cuts, scrapes, limping → First aid on-site
  • Moderate: Bleeding, visible wounds, pain → Contact owner, may need vet
  • Severe: Unconscious, severe bleeding, broken bones → Emergency vet immediately

Step 3: Contact Dog Owner

  • Immediate contact for any injury
  • Provide clear information about what happened
  • Get permission for veterinary care if needed
  • Document owner's response and instructions

Step 4: Veterinary Care

  • Emergency vet: Transport immediately if severe
  • Regular vet: Schedule appointment if moderate
  • Owner's vet: Contact if owner requests
  • Document all veterinary visits and costs

3. Severe Weather Protocols

Tornado/Windstorm Warning

Pre-Storm Preparation

  • Monitor weather alerts via NOAA radio or weather app
  • Secure outdoor equipment and furniture
  • Close and lock all windows and doors
  • Move dogs to interior rooms away from windows
During Warning
  • Immediate Action: Evacuate to designated safe area (interior room, basement, or storm shelter)
  • Bring all dogs to safe area
  • Account for all staff and customers
  • Stay away from windows and exterior walls
  • Remain in safe area until warning expires

Extreme Heat

Heat Advisory (90°F+)

  • Limit outdoor play time to early morning/evening
  • Provide extra water stations throughout facility
  • Monitor dogs for heat stress (excessive panting, lethargy, drooling)
  • Reduce activity levels and provide cooling areas
  • Close facility if indoor temperature exceeds safe levels (85°F+)
Heat Stroke Response
  • Immediate cooling: Move dog to cool area, apply cool (not cold) water
  • Contact emergency vet immediately
  • Monitor vital signs while transporting
  • Document incident and notify owner

4. Facility Issues & Evacuation

Fire Emergency

Discovery of Fire
  1. Pull fire alarm immediately
  2. Call 911 - Provide exact address and nature of fire
  3. Evacuate facility - Use nearest exit, do not use elevators
  4. Account for all people at designated meeting point
  5. DO NOT re-enter building until fire department clears it

Evacuation Procedure

  • Alert everyone: "FIRE - EVACUATE NOW"
  • Grab leashes and secure dogs quickly
  • Use nearest exit - Know all exit locations
  • Assemble at meeting point (designated safe area)
  • Account for all staff and customers
  • Do NOT attempt to save property - Life safety first

5. Dog-Related Incidents

Dog Fight

Immediate Response
  1. DO NOT use hands to separate dogs
  2. Use distraction - Loud noise, water spray, blanket
  3. Separate dogs using barriers or leashes
  4. Assess injuries to both dogs
  5. Secure both dogs in separate areas

Post-Incident

  • Document incident thoroughly
  • Contact both dog owners immediately
  • Provide veterinary care if needed
  • Review incident with staff
  • Update dog compatibility records

6. Medical Emergency Procedures

Unconscious Person

  1. Check for responsiveness - Tap shoulder, call name
  2. Call 911 immediately
  3. Check for breathing - Look, listen, feel
  4. Begin CPR if trained and not breathing
  5. Use AED if available and trained
  6. Stay with person until EMS arrives

9. Communication Protocols

During Emergency

  • Designate spokesperson - One person communicates with emergency services
  • Use clear, calm language - Avoid panic
  • Follow chain of command - Manager → Owner → Insurance

Post-Emergency Communication

Within 24 Hours:
  • Contact affected parties - Customers, staff, dog owners
  • Notify insurance - Follow policy requirements
  • File incident reports - Complete all documentation
  • Internal review - Staff meeting to discuss incident

10. Post-Incident Procedures

Immediate (Within 1 Hour):
  • Secure scene and preserve evidence
  • Complete incident report forms
  • Take photos of scene and injuries
  • Collect witness statements
  • Notify management and owner
  • Contact emergency services if needed
Short-Term (Within 24 Hours):
  • Notify insurance company
  • Contact all affected parties
  • Review security footage (if available)
  • Conduct staff debriefing
  • Assess facility safety
  • Determine if facility closure needed
