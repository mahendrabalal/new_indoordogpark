## Introduction: Keeping Your Pup Active in C-Bus

Columbus, Ohio, is known for its Buckeyes and its burgeoning tech scene, but let's be real—it's also known for its unpredictable weather. From muggy summer thunderstorms to freezing winter snow (and that famous gray slush), keeping a high-energy dog entertained can be a challenge.

According to local climate data, Columbus gets its fair share of precipitation and cold days. When the Scioto Mile is frozen over or Highbanks Metro Park is a mud pit, you need a backup plan.

The solution? **Indoor dog parks.** These climate-controlled havens are becoming a staple in Central Ohio. We've rounded up the best spots in the 614 where you can grab a drink, socialize, and let your dog burn off that Zoomie energy, all while staying dry.

## Know Before You Go: Indoor Park Etiquette

The rules in C-Bus are similar to indoor parks everywhere, but it pays to be prepared:

1.  **Vaccinations are Non-Negotiable:** Expect to provide proof of Rabies, Distemper/Parvo, and Bordetella.
2.  **Temperament Tests:** Most membership-based places (like Bumble's) will require a "meet and greet" or temperament evaluation before your first unchaperoned play session.
3.  **Spay/Neuter:** Intact dogs (especially males over 6 months) are often restricted.
4.  **Clean Up:** While staff is often around, you are ultimately responsible for scooping your dog's poop. Bags are always provided!
