# Pinterest Growth Strategy for Indoor Dog Park

Pinterest is a visual discovery engine. For the dog niche, it is a goldmine for driving high-intent traffic from the USA.

## 1. Profile Setup
- **Business Account**: Ensure you are using a Business account to see analytics.
- **Keywords**: Use keywords like "Dog Care", "Dog Parks", "Pet Travel", and "Puppy Tips" in your bio.

## 2. Board Structure
Create the following boards to organize your content:
1. **Best Indoor Dog Parks (USA)**: Focus on your city-specific pages.
2. **Dog Travel Safety**: Link to your blog post about dog cages/carriers.
3. **Winter Dog Activities**: Focus on the benefit of *indoor* parks.
4. **Dog Training & Play**: General engagement content.

## 3. Pin Design Principles
- **Ratio**: Always use **2:3 aspect ratio** (e.g., 1000 x 1500 pixels).
- **Text Overlay**: Use bold, readable text that solves a problem (e.g., "Top 5 Indoor Parks in NYC").
- **Branding**: Include your logo or `indoordogpark.org` URL at the bottom of every pin.
- **Colors**: Use warm, "pet-friendly" colors (oranges, blues, and purples).

## 4. Initial Pin Schedule
I will generate assets for these high-priority pins:
1. **Guide to Dog Cages in Cars** (Blog Post)
2. **Best Indoor Dog Parks in NYC** (City Page)
3. **Best Indoor Dog Parks in Houston** (City Page)

## 5. Automation & Consistency
- Aim for **5-10 pins per day** (can be a mix of your content and repinning others).
- Use a tool like **Tailwind** or Pinterest's built-in scheduler to save time.
